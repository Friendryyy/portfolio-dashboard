### 📋 Financial Data — Complete Picture

**FY2025 Full Year:**
- Revenue: $70.9M (primarily from MNO test agreements, not commercial service)
- FCF: **-$1.24B** — massive cash burn (pre-revenue phase)
- Cash: $2.78B (raised through multiple equity offerings)

- Net cash position: ~$540M — narrow runway

**Burn Rate Analysis:**
- Operating cash burn ~$250-300M/quarter
- At current rate: ~5-6 quarters of runway without additional fundraising
- Expected to raise more capital before commercial launch

### 🛰️ Satellite Deployment Status

**BlueBird Program — Current Status:**
- Target: 60 satellites for commercial service
- Deployed: 7 BlueBirds (BB1-BB6 = Block 1 test sats; BB7 = LOST)
- BlueBird 7: **LOST on April 19, 2026** — New Glenn rocket (Blue Origin) failure at launch
- Next Launch: **Falcon 9 mid-June 2026** — BlueBirds 8, 9, 10 (Block 2, larger capacity)
- Block 2 specs: higher capacity per satellite, fewer total needed for commercial coverage

**FCC Regulatory Status:**
- FCC approved ASTS to operate **248 satellites** in LEO
- Regulatory moat: getting FCC approval is multi-year process — barrier to entry

### 📡 Commercial Partnerships

**Mobile Network Operator (MNO) Partners:**
- **50+ MNO partners** signed — AT&T, Verizon (US), Vodafone (Europe), Rakuten (Japan)
- Total subscriber reach: **3+ billion** (via partner networks)
- Revenue model: wholesale to MNOs, not direct-to-consumer

**Alphabet (Google) Stake:**
- Google holds strategic investment in ASTS
- SpaceX IPO risk: if SpaceX goes public and offers Direct-to-Cell service, could create investor comparison pressure

**Ligado Spectrum Deal:**
- $550M spectrum deal for additional L-band spectrum (pending FCC regulatory approval)
- L-band เพิ่มความสามารถในการให้บริการในพื้นที่ที่มีสัญญาณแออัด — สำคัญสำหรับ urban coverage

**Alphabet (Google) Strategic Investment:**
- Google ถือหุ้น ASTS เชิงกลยุทธ์ — alignment ระหว่าง Google connectivity mission กับ ASTS TAM
- หากสำเร็จ = Google ได้ประโยชน์จาก 3B+ users ที่เชื่อมต่อได้ผ่าน Android

### 🌐 Technology Differentiation

**vs SpaceX Starlink Direct-to-Cell:**
- Starlink: custom modem required / slow data speeds initially
- ASTS: works with **existing unmodified smartphones** (standard SIM) — key differentiator
- Latency: ASTS LEO (700km) vs Starlink LEO (550km) — comparable

**vs Apple Emergency SOS (Globalstar/Iridium):**
- Current satellite phones: text/emergency SOS only, very slow
- ASTS target: full broadband speeds (MB/s) for voice, video, data
